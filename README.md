# Arewa 🌸
### *Your body is not a mystery — it's a masterpiece.*
### *Welcome to the most important relationship you'll ever have.*

**Arewa** (Àrẹwà) means *beautiful woman* in Yoruba — because every African woman who understands her body is exactly that.

Arewa is a pan-African women's cycle and wellness app designed specifically for African women — honouring our foods, our languages, our conditions, and our stories. Built by a Nigerian pharmacology graduate. Powered by real science.

🔗 **Live app:** [https://tomeehstar.github.io/arewa](https://tomeehstar.github.io/arewa)

---

## What is Arewa?

Most period apps were built for Western women. They don't know what eba is, they don't track PCOS the way it presents in African women, they don't speak Yoruba, Igbo, or Hausa, and they don't understand what it means to fast during Ramadan while managing cramps.

Arewa does.

It is a mobile-first digital health companion that combines clinical accuracy with cultural relevance — built as a portfolio prototype by a pharmacology graduate transitioning into digital health.

## Meet Tomi 💕

Tomi is Arewa's AI cycle companion — named after the pharmacology graduate who built the app.

She is warm, knowledgeable, and a girl's girl. She gives you real health information grounded in pharmacology — and she always reminds you she is a companion, not a replacement for your doctor or pharmacist.

Tomi is powered by the Claude API (Anthropic). She speaks to you like a best friend who happens to know exactly what's happening in your body.

**Example questions you can ask Tomi:**
- *"Why do I get so moody before my period?"*
- *"Is it safe to take ibuprofen every month for cramps?"*
- *"What is PCOS and how do I know if I have it?"*
- *"Can I exercise during my period?"*
- *"What foods help with bloating?"*

## Why Arewa Exists

> *PCOS affects 1 in 10 African women. Endometriosis affects 1 in 7. Both are chronically underdiagnosed. Most period apps offer no cultural context, no African foods, and no awareness of how these conditions present differently in African bodies.*

Arewa was built to close that gap — starting with a prototype, growing into a platform.

## Design Principles

- **Mobile-first only** — built for how African women actually use their phones
- **Apple & Android compliant** — Dynamic Island status bar, 44px touch targets, `env(safe-area-inset-bottom)` safe zones
- **Offline-capable core** — Calendar, Tracker, Myth-Buster, Nutrition work without internet
- **Culturally specific** — not a Western app with African stickers added on top
